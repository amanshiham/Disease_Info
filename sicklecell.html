Overview

Sickle cell anemia is a genetic disorder that causes red blood cells to become misshapen and break down. This can lead to various health issues, including severe pain and organ damage.

It is a chronic condition that requires ongoing medical care to manage symptoms and prevent complications. With proper treatment, many people with sickle cell anemia can lead active and fulfilling lives.

Symptoms

  • Severe pain in the chest, abdomen, or joints
  • Fatigue
  • Shortness of breath
  • Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
  • Frequent infections
  • Delayed growth and development

Causes

Sickle cell anemia is caused by a mutation in the HBB gene that codes for hemoglobin. This mutation leads to the production of abnormal hemoglobin, which causes red blood cells to become misshapen and break down.

The condition is inherited in an autosomal recessive pattern, meaning that a person must inherit two copies of the mutated gene (one from each parent) to develop the condition.

Treatment

Treatment for sickle cell anemia typically involves managing symptoms and preventing complications. This may include:

  • Pain management with medication
  • Antibiotics to prevent infections
  • Hydroxyurea to increase fetal hemoglobin production
  • Blood transfusions to increase red blood cell count
  • Stem cell transplantation to replace abnormal stem cells
